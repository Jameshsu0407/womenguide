Taipei Xia Hia City God Temple

Introduce

Xiahai Town God temple, founded in 1859, is an important belief center for people in the Area of Dadaocheng. The main worship was the town god of Xiahaei, and the main god in the side temple was lady City God. The side temple was also dominated by female gods, so it also became the spiritual sustenance of local women. In the past, women in Dadaocheng used to go to the town God temple to pray for the blessing of the city God's Wife. Since then, whenever a woman wished for the happiness of her family, she would present embroidered shoes to show her gratitude. This was also the origin of the city God's Wife's shoes. Up to now, both sides have independence and equal status equally in marriage, because this offer asks a pair of lady shoes to go back, in addition to symbol the lady to bless the whole family in the home safe, also let the member in the home each other tolerance, respect, feeling harmonious and happy.

Yongle Fabric Market

Introduce

The original nature of the market was to sell food and snacks, but because of its proximity to the Daidaocheng dock, Japanese traders used this place as a wholesale center for cloth imports. At that time, more than half of the cloth traders in Taiwan gathered in Daidaocheng.

However, when the stores needed labor, the women of the family assisted the men, which was the most common female role in the daidaocheng businesses. This kind of double labor is women's work, which is to play the role of wife and mother in the wholesale and retail stores. On the other hand, they still have to serve as employees, but also have to take care of guests and staff meals. After the war, however, women were able to participate in or take charge of the stores, gradually reversing the traditional role of women and further changing the original relationship between the sexes.

former site of Kangle Hospital／Daqian Department

Introduce

Opened in 1970, the store introduced an electric escalator to attract an endless stream of pilgrims, creating numerous business opportunities for dadaocheng.

Formerly known as Kangle Hospital, it was established by Hsieh E, the first female surgeon in Taiwan. Because of her high profile, the hospital was often crowded with guests; The downstairs was a hospital, and the upstairs was a gathering place for the political and business elites of the time, especially young people, students and women's activists. She also took an active part in the women's movement, first establishing the Taipei City Women's Association and then preparing for the Taiwan Women's Association, the first provincial women's organization after the war. Therefore, he is known as the "pusher of Taiwan women's movement". At the end of 1947, she was elected as a legislator and was the first female legislator in Taiwan. Undoubtedly, she was the most brilliant female political star at that time.

former site of Daan Hospital

Introduce

In 1921, Jiang Weishui founded the "Taiwan Cultural Association" in Da 'an Hospital. Under the influence of the traditional patriarchal concept of respecting men and giving priority to husbands, Women in Taiwan were oppressed and their status and status could not be treated reasonably and equally. Therefore, "Taiwan Cultural Association" also accepted women members.

Jiang's ideas were advanced, and his goal was to "affirm the principle of equality between men and women, aid the feminist movement, and oppose human trafficking". In 1924, "Women's Health" was serialized in "Taiwan Minbao", which was a set of quite detailed women's health manual. It was also the first time for Taiwan media to pay attention to women's bodies, educate women how to understand and take care of their own bodies, and break the traditional impression that women's bodies were regarded as dirty and shameful.

Dadaocheng women’s Elementary School (during Japanese colonial period)

Introduce

Founded in 1898, it was originally the "Girls' Department of Dadaocheng Public School", but the number of students increased year by year. In 1911, it became the independent "Girls' School of Dadaocheng". In 1922, it had cultivated nearly 700 graduates.

It was the first public school for girls in Taipei, which is now Penglai Primary School. In Taiwan, girls' education was not introduced into the school system until the Japanese rule. The goal of girls' education was to create a good wife and a good mother who could take care of the family. Therefore, girls had to learn various crafts such as tailoring, embroidery and cooking, as well as family lessons.

Cimin Temple（Dimu Niangniang）

Introduce

Cimin Palace, founded in 1932, is the first earth mother temple in Yilan. The founder, Ms. Yang Li A Kam, gave her daughter away shortly after she was born. Due to the difficulties in her marriage life, she decided to cultivate herself. One day, the mother of the earth had a dream, so she left her husband's house with her daughter and moved into a guardhouse of the size of three ping on the bank of the Yilan River. In the early days, the earth mother statue was placed in other temples. Thanks to the support of rich families and many believers, the statue was purchased with donations and moved to the present site around 1953.

former site of Qichang Hall

Introduce

In the Qing Dynasty, there were strict regulations for women to become nuns. In order to settle unmarried, widowed or disabled women and avoid trouble, the gentry of Yilan funded the construction of zhaitang (commonly known as Caitang), which served as a place for family women to cultivate their morality and live for the aged. In this time and space, Qichang Hall was established in 1825.

Due to the change of regime, the name was changed to Lei Yin Temple in the late period of Japanese rule. In 1953, it was changed to Yilan Buddhist Prayer Association, and now it is called The Lanyang Temple of Fo Guang Mountain. Although the name has changed again and again, and the believers have different beliefs, its followers are still mainly women.

Human Rights Monument

Introduce

In the Qing Dynasty, it was a custom for The Han people to keep concubines and menservants. The menservants were of low status, especially those commonly known as "TSA-boo-kan". Most of them were imprisoned for life. Qing dynasty officials see this evil custom violation of human rights, repeatedly ordered to improve, and even offered out of the rod of the regulations, but still can not be banned. In 1840, The General court of Yilan ordered Xu Tinglun to erect a new monument to warn him, with the name "The Kamalan Hall is forbidden to raise maidservants until they are 25 years old." This monument was originally in front of the Gamalan Office, and was excavated in zhongshan Park during the Japanese rule period. It is now in Yilan Cultural Center.

former site of Yilan women’s Elementary School (during Japanese colonial period)

Introduce

In the early days of Japanese rule, public school education was promoted, requiring both boys and girls to receive basic education. However, due to the persistence of footbinding and the shackles of traditional concepts, few girls went to school. It was not until 1918 that the number of girls in Yilan Public School reached the standard of establishing an independent school. In that time, Yilan Public School for Girls was established, which was the first school exclusively for girls in Yilan. At that time, however, the goal of education was to train women to be tailors and domestic workers. It was not until 1996 that the school was officially closed for girls and was renamed as yilan National Primary School.

former site of lanyang women’s High School (during Japanese colonial period)

Introduce

During the period of Japanese rule, yilan was inconvenient to travel to other places. Those who wanted to study for high school girls had to go to Taipei and Keelung, which was very inconvenient. Under the constraints of economic conditions and the preference for sons over daughters, only a small number of children of the gentry were able to achieve their wishes. In 1938, the Taipei Lanyang State High School for Women was finally established. After the war, the school was renamed Taiwan Lanyang Girls' Senior High School and is now national Lanyang Girls' Senior High School. The gate is the only remaining facility of the school, which was designated as a county historic site in 2011.
